Top 5 AI Tools Everyone Should Know in 2025

Explore AI apps that can improve creativity, research, and productivity in school, work, and beyond.

Published: September 19, 2025 • Nextoria

1. ChatGPT (Large Language Models)

Still the most popular conversational AI, ChatGPT helps with drafting text, answering questions, brainstorming, and learning new concepts.

Use-cases: essay drafts, code snippets, research summaries, lesson prep.

Prompt example: "Explain blockchain in simple terms for a 12th grade student, using a real-world analogy."

2. Claude

Claude, from Anthropic, is designed to be safe, helpful, and conversational. It handles longer documents and detailed instructions better than many peers.

Use-cases: summarizing large PDFs, customer support chat, generating structured outputs.

3. MidJourney (AI Image Generation)

MidJourney creates stunning visuals from text prompts. It’s widely used for design, marketing, education, and creative projects.

Use-cases: social media graphics, presentation images, book illustrations.

Prompt example: "A futuristic classroom with students using AI-powered tablets, digital painting style."

4. n8n (Automation Platform)

n8n connects apps and automates workflows without heavy coding. Perfect for automating repetitive tasks and boosting productivity.

Use-cases: form submissions → Google Sheets → email confirmations.

5. Notion AI

Notion AI brings AI-powered writing, summarization, and task assistance into the popular productivity platform Notion.

Use-cases: meeting notes, project summaries, idea generation.

Final Thoughts

These five tools — ChatGPT, Claude, MidJourney, n8n, and Notion AI — represent the core of how AI is used in 2025: writing, research, creativity, automation, and productivity. By combining them, learners and professionals can save hours of work and focus on innovation.
